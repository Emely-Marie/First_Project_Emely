Introduction to Art!

There are so many types of art that can range from realism, to the form of art Picasso was best known for: cubism! No matter what your preferred art style is, art surrounds us all and is something to be appreciated.

This website is about two very different styles of painting: Cubism and Impressionism!

This is a cubism type of art piece by Pablo Picasso titled "The Weeping Woman," painted with oil paint on canvas in 1937. He began painting this after seeing photos of the Luftwaffe's bombing in Guernica during the Spanish civil war on April 26, 1937. The painting was created, and Picasso insisted we imagine ourselves in the face of the weeping woman, in an act of protest against the war. Artists abondened the idea of realism and instead decided to explore different forms of art, thus began the era of

Cubism!

Probably the most famous impresionist painting is The Starry Night by Vincent van Gogh. This was painted in 1889, and was also painted with oil on canvas. It was painted in his hospital room at Saint-Remy-de-Provence, he was facing east as he watched the sunrise. Van Gogh was not allowed to paint in his room, so he sketched what he saw outside of his window for many days; in the rain, morning, and evening. He then painted over 20 variations of this scene, including The Starry Night.

These artists known as impressionists inspired a whole community to go against the government in an art style called

Impressionism!
